    🥥 A coconut seller just taught the most powerful marketing lesson of the year.

    No MBA. No marketing team. No ad spend.

    Just a handwritten board in Bengaluru that said the following:

    Zepto — ₹80
    Blinkit — ₹80
    BigBasket — ₹70
    Our Price — Only ₹55

    That's it. That's the whole strategy.

    And it's brilliant — here's why:

    1. He knew his business environment.
    Before pricing, he studied the competition. He didn't guess. He observed. Most businesses never do this—they price in a vacuum.

    2. He communicated with radical clarity.
    No jargon. No fancy tagline. One board, five lines, one undeniable truth: you're paying more for the same thing somewhere else.

    3. He turned a weakness into a weapon.
    Street vendors are often considered "less convenient" than apps. He flipped the narrative—the apps are just more expensive.

    4. He understood what his customer was already thinking.
    "Should I order online or go to the street stall?" He answered that question before they even asked it.

    This is what good marketing really is:

    ✅ Understand your competitive landscape
    ✅ Know your value proposition
    ✅ Speak your customer's language
    ✅ Make the choice obvious

    You don't need a six-figure campaign to win market share. You need awareness of your environment and the courage to communicate clearly.

    The best marketing doesn't feel like marketing. It just feels like the right choice.
